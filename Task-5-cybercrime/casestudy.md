# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing scenario begins with a fake email or message that appears to be from a bank or a popular app. The message usually creates urgency, such as claiming that the user’s account will be blocked. It then provides a link that looks genuine. When the victim clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website designed to look like the real one. The victim enters their login details or banking information, which is directly captured by the attacker. In some cases, the attacker may also call the victim pretending to be a bank official and ask for OTPs.

College students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may not verify sources carefully.

The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may also face stress and loss of trust in online systems. This highlights the importance of awareness and cautious online behavior.
